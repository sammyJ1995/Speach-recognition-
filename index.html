History of Halo

Halo is series of mainly first-person shooter video games starring modified super soldier known as Spartan John 117 aka Master chief and in Halo 2 he was joined by an Alien Elite named The Arbiter and in Halo 5 another Spartan known as Spartan Locke, In Halo Reach and ODST you play as Noble 6 and The Rookie respectfully.

Halo was first announced at MacWorld conference originally as RTS style game but through development had turned into a large-scale team-based shooter like Tribes or Battlefield and was planned for release on the Mac and Windows. Microsoft eventually claimed exclusive rights to Bungie and Halo franchise. The development of Halo switch# to the Xbox as an exclusive and was released alongside the Xbox in 2001. Halo Combat Evolved was huge success for the Xbox selling over 4 million copies and by 2003 Bungie announced the sequel Halo 2 at E3 2003 and was announced to be released late 2004. Halo 2 Continued where Halo Combat Evolved as huge success selling over 8 million copies and introduces Xbox live aka online gaming to the Halo series.

Bungie then started development for the sequel of Halo 2, Halo 3 was announced in 2006 for the Xbox 360 to be released in 2007. After Halo 3, Bungie bought its independence back from Microsoft and made a deal to produce two more games under the publisher. Halo 3: ODST and Halo: Reach released 2009 and 2010 respectively, were Bungie's last games in the series. In 2009 Halo wars was released for the Xbox 360 it was a RTS game and the first Halo game to be released by a company other the bungie (Ensembled studios). After Bungie left Microsoft a new company was put together by Microsoft to make future Halo games this company is known as 343 Industries. 343 released there first game in 2011 which was a remaster of Halo Combat Evolved followed by Halo 4 in 2012. 343 along with a few other companies release Halo the Master chief Collection which was collections featuring Halo Combat Evolved Anniversary, the brand new Halo 2 Anniversary, Halo 3 and Halo 4. In 2015 343 released Halo 5 Guardians on the Xbox One and in 2019 343 announced Halo Infinite for there brand-new Xbox releasing in 2020.
